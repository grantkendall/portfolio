"Improvised Landscapes," 2024, zine

Created during my last year of undergrad at UMKC, this zine is an exploration in how to translate layered digital worlds into a printed format. The translucency of vellum paper was chosen to try and simulate the leakage of data and attention; images never come into focus. Black ink from an inkjet print added a layer of physical compression to the images, flattening and further obscuring their sources.

Perennial Institute, design ideation

Design mock-ups, sketches and ideas for the Perennial Institute, a creative retreat held in Berlin. The excerpt of work here is primarily concerned with the program's identity and layout for an educational deck of cards.

"A visual representation of letter usage," 2017, book

Typography as map... Linguistics as statistics... A book as a pure container for information. Vellum, spiral-bound book with hand-cut circles that represent the frequency of letter usage in the English language. It resides in the permanent Artists' Book Collection at the Minneapolis College of Art and Design Library after winning 1st prize in the 2018 Student Artist's Book Competition.

"Dissent/Descent," 2017, enamel on mirror

Part protest piece, part linguistic exploration. My first explorations with conceptual artworks began in the space of puzzles. Standing in front of the work, the viewer's head is hidden in the black void, creating a temporary lack of identity.

"Blooms," 2017, zine

I learned about the phenomenon of jellyfish blooms while working with a flatbed scanner as a tool for image-making. Manipulating mundane objects during the scanning process yielded images with iridescent artifacts, invoking jellyfish suspended in dark waters. This zine is a collection of the most striking images.

Selected Paintings, 2016-2025

Selected paintings that span nearly a decade and explore diverse and changing subject matter. I paint on a variety of surfaces -- canvas, wood, found materials -- and use oils, acrylic or gouache.

Selected Prints

A selection of prints made during my time as a student in MCAD's Print Paper Book tract. The prints were made using differing methods including silkscreen, stencil and woodblock.
